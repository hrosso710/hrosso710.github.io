About me:

I am a fifth-year Ph.D. student in the scientific computing group at Emory University. I work with Talea Mayo and Lars Ruthotto. My research interests include uncertainty quantification, surge modeling, continuous-time deep neural networks, and simulation-based inference.

I was an intern at Sandia National Labs from Spring 2023 to Fall 2024, where I worked under Bekah White on sensitivity analysis and uncertainty quantification.

I also am the president of Emory's SIAM student chapter and am a facilitator for the queer grads community group hosted by the office of LGBT life.

I'm from Houston, TX and graduated from the University of Houston in 2020 after transfering from community college. There, I worked with Andreas Mang and James Herring on inverse problems research.

In my free time, I'm usually hanging out with my cats, exploring Atlanta, walking around, or doing a puzzle or a random project of some kind...
